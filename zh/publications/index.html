  1. Under Review
    DiverseSAT: Finding k Diverse Satisfying Assignments via Threshold Encodings and Incremental Solving
    Zhengling Yangli and others
    Under Review, 2026
    Submitted to JAIR
    Abs PDF

    We study the problem of computing k maximally diverse satisfying assignments of a propositional formula, a setting with applications to bounded model checking (e.g., all-zero / all-one witnesses) and robust network path design. We propose two encodings — Direct Weight (DW) and Incremental Weight with incremental totalizer (IW) — and three search strategies (binary-lifting, binary-search, SAT-UNSAT), together with a model-aware refinement that exploits partial solutions. Experiments on 289 instances across 7 benchmark families show that our approach consistently outperforms MaxSAT, Pseudo-Boolean and CPLEX baselines.

  2. Working Paper
    Encoding Non-linear Integer Programs into Weighted MaxSAT: A Systematic Study
    Zhengling Yangli and others
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abs

    We investigate the encoding of non-linear integer programs (NLIP) — polynomial objectives and constraints over bounded integer variables — into weighted MaxSAT. Four encoding families are studied (one-hot, unary, binary, and binary with order decomposition) and evaluated on QPLIB (137), SMT-LIB QF_NIA (2591) and Diverse-SAT (287) benchmarks against Z3, CPLEX and CaDiCaL baselines. We identify when MaxSAT-based encoding dominates and diagnose a fundamental limitation of unary encoding at large domain sizes.

  3. Working Paper
    Ant-Q: A Lightweight Ant-Colony + Q-Learning Plug-in that Tightens Lower Bounds for Minimum Weight Dominating Set
    Zhengling Yangli and others
    Working Paper, 2026
    Built on ECAI-2025 baselines
    Abs

    Building on the Dual-Bound Search (DBS) framework of the ECAI 2025 baselines Dual-Deep and Dual-Fast, we plug a K-ant Ant-Q module into the LB-side pair construction of ibmwds_init_bounds, without touching the UB search. The resulting variants Deep-v6 and Fast-v19 reduce the row-averaged gap by 23.68%/31.23% (T1/T2) on Deep and by 6.92%/9.89% (T1/T2) on Fast, with instance-level win rates up to 99.37%. A central finding is the asymmetry between gap and #opt: tightening LB alone does not shrink UB unless it triggers hard reduction rules.

  4. Working Paper
    Beyond DRF-MT: Group-Level Differentiation for Fair and Efficient Multi-Resource Allocation under Meta-Type Accessibility
    Zhengling Yangli and others
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abs

    DRF-MT (Yin et al., IJCAI 2021) guarantees share-incentive, envy-freeness, Pareto-optimality and strategy-proofness, but forces all active tenants to share the same dominant share y within each elimination round. We propose UNB-MT, which partitions tenants by dominant meta-type into a majority and minority group, compresses the majority at a tunable y_\textbase, and lets the minority grow further via DRF-MT on residual resources. We prove SI, intra-group EF and minority-to-majority EF, and show +2%–6% average social welfare gain over DRF-MT on meta-type benchmarks. We also analyze why a linear EF sufficient condition derived from Lemma 2 is too loose under partial access, and discuss a cutting-plane alternative.

  5. Working Paper
    MathRAG: A Retrieval-Augmented Pipeline for Step-wise Mathematical Problem Solving
    Zhengling Yangli and others
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abs

    MathRAG is a retrieval-augmented mathematical QA system that couples a math-aware retriever with a Qwen2-Math backbone and a step-wise verification layer. The system reaches \sim94% on GSM8K and \sim89% on MATH; we report a reproducible pipeline spanning problem comprehension, LaTeX-preserving retrieval, chain-of-thought generation and step-level answer validation, together with a lightweight web application (Vue frontend, Node.js + Redis backend).
